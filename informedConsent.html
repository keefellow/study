<h1> Mindful Affirmation Practice Mobile App Study (COVID-19)

NTU-IRB Ref no. IRB-2020-04-027 </h1>

</font><font size="+20"></font><font size="+20">

<p> You are invited to participate in a research study on how

practice of mindful affirmations via a mobile app may have an

effect on your perceptions during this COVID-19 pandemic. This study

is conducted by <b>Assistant Professor Kee Ying Hwa, National

Institute of Education, Singapore at the Nanyang Technological

University</b>. <br>

<br>

This study will be conducted <b>over 8 days</b>, which will

involve <b>using the designated app to record the frequency of

practice of mindful affirmations</b>, as well as </b> responding to

questionnaires twice</b>. <br>

<br>

Before beginning the study, we ask that you first think of <b>a

phrase that you believe is personally meaningful</b>, something which

can be used as an verbal affirmation that can help you feel more

positive in this COVID-19 situation. For example, this

affirmation may be short phrases like <b>“Think positive”</b>, <b>“This too shall

pass”</b>, <b>“We will overcome this”</b>, or any phrase,

mantra or invocations that you want to focus on. 

This affirmation should be a phrase you are willing to repeated aloud or silently at least between 20 to 

  500 times per day, over these next 7 days.

You can also use a simple phrase like <b>“I notice my breathe”</b> as an

affirmation, which may help to facilitate your practice of

mindful breathing when you say it repeatedly. <br>

<br>

Whatever phrase you decide on as your affirmation, while you repeat the

affirmation verbally (silently or aloud), you are asked to <b>mindfully

tap on the counting button on the app</b>. As you coordinate

your verbalisation with your hand action during this task, such

practice may bring about a sense of mindful focus and

attention, which in turn may have some effects on your well-being. To

help you stay motivated, we also provide a chart within the app

to help you keep track of your progress over the seven days. <b>

You will be given a minimum daily target to meet, which can be between

20 to 500 counts</b>. 

<br>

In addition to the data we collect from your mindful affirmation

practice, you will also be asked to complete <b>two online

surveys on Day 1 and Day 8</b>. In particular, we kindly

request that you remember to complete the survey on Day 8,

regardless of your practice frequency. This will help improve

the validity of this study. <br>

<br>

A target of <b>1000</b> participants are targeted for this study. <br>

<br>

Your participation in this study is completely voluntary, and

you have the right to withdraw from the study at any time

without any penalty. You may skip also any questions you do not

wish to answer. If you do not wish to complete the study, simply

delete the mobile application. <br>

<br>

Your participation in this research will be kept confidential.

Your responses will be anonymous and IP addresses will not be

collected to guarantee complete anonymity. Possible outlets of

dissemination may be journals related to public health,

mindfulness, and mhealth. <br>

<br>

Kindly note that anonymised and non-identified data collected

from this study may be published in open data depository under

the University’s data sharing guidelines. <br>

<br>

Although your participation in this research may not benefit you

personally, it will help us understand how such affirmative

practices can improve psychological well-being during this

period of COVID-19 pandemic. There will be no compensation

provided. <br>

<br>

There are no risks to individuals participating in this survey

beyond those that exists in daily life. <br>

<br>

We will provide <b>a brief report</b> of your participation for reference on the "About U" section of the app at the end of the study. <br>

<br>    

If you have questions about this project, you may contact

<b>Assistant Professor Kee Ying Hwa (contact information +65

 67903374</b> and <b>yinghwa.kee@nie.edu.sg</b>). 

 You may also communicate with us anonymously using the "Contact Us" feature of the app.<br>

</p>
